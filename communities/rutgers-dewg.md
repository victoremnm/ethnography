# Rutgers Digital Ethnography Working Group (DEWG)

## The Thesis: "Institutionalizing the Digital Gaze"

In 2026, the **Rutgers DEWG** serves as the academic vanguard for understanding "how people live today" through digital media. While it positions itself as a center for ethical, multimodal research, it represents the **institutionalization of surveillance**—documenting the radical fringe to maintain the university's role as the "Primary Observer."

- **The Primary Shift:** From "being there" (physical) to **"Digital Qualitative Fluidity"**—where AI tools and datafication dissolve the boundaries between traditional interviewing and digital observation (Lane, 2026).
- **Core Focus:** Navigating the "processes, practices, and boundaries" around generative AI in fieldwork (Lane & Rivera, 2026).

---

## Key 2026 Nodes & Frameworks

### 1. Algorithmic Folklore (The ALGOFOLK Project)
Led by Gabriele de Seta, this 2026 framework operationalizes the idea that automated systems give rise to new kinds of **vernacular creativity**. It emphasizes the **"Mutual Shaping"** of humans and machines (de Seta, 2026).
- **AI Cryptids:** Studying entities like "Loab" that emerge from the latent space of generative models.
- **Folk Forensics:** Vernacular practices used to identify "AI-ness" or "slop" aesthetics in digital artifacts.
- **Synthetic Vernaculars:** Linguistic and visual styles (e.g., "Italian Brainrot") optimized specifically for algorithmic circulation.

### 2. Corporate Open Science (COS)
Presented by Zhuofan Li (2026), this critique examines how major tech companies use platforms like **arXiv** and **GitHub** as **"Relational Infrastructure"** to exert corporate power.
- **Brokerage and Control:** Corporations act as "brokers" between academic research and commercial application, steering the direction of AI development.
- **The AI Hype Paradox:** Releasing "open" models to fuel public hype while consolidating control over the underlying data and compute resources.

### 3. The "Scientific Monoculture" Warning
A 2026 inquiry into how generative AI is homogenizing scientific methods and topics, turning research into a "scientific monoculture" (Li, 2026).

## Contested Views: Building Around the Periphery

*Applying the "Adversarial Fact-Checking" protocol:*

### The "Museum of Digital Life" Risk
Rutgers DEWG documents digital cultures, but remains bound by the bureaucratic hierarchies of the university.
- **Fact-Check:** Academic peer-review cycles (6-18 months) are fundamentally misaligned with the real-time "Magma" of CT and Farcaster.
- **Refute:** DEWG is creating a **museum of the present.** It documents the decline of the old world while being unable to act within the new "Sovereign" world of Network States.

### The "Decoloniality" Performance
- **The Critique:** Skeptics argue that "digital ethnography" within Western university structures is fundamentally **data extraction** with an empathetic vocabulary. It fails to challenge the underlying power dynamics of the platforms it studies.
