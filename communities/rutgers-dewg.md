# Rutgers Digital Ethnography Working Group (DEWG)

## The Thesis: "Institutionalizing the Digital Gaze"

In 2026, the **Rutgers DEWG** serves as the academic vanguard for understanding "how people live today" through digital media. While it positions itself as a center for ethical, multimodal research, it represents the **institutionalization of surveillance**—documenting the radical fringe to maintain the university's role as the "Primary Observer."

- **The Primary Shift:** From "being there" (physical) to **"Algorithmic Folklore"** (Gabriele de Seta, 2026).
- **Core Focus:** Navigating the "processes, practices, and boundaries" around generative AI in fieldwork (Lane & Rivera, 2026).

---

## Key 2026 Nodes & Events

### 1. ALGOFOLK Project
Led by Gabriele de Seta, this 2026 initiative examines the "mutual shaping of vernacular creativity and automation." It investigates how LLMs and recommendation engines don't just "observe" culture but actively **author** new forms of algorithmic folklore.

### 2. "Risky Research" Protocols
A major 2026 focus on "doing risky research safely."
- **The Paradox:** Critics argue this focus on "safety" and "ethics boards" is a mechanism for **academic gatekeeping**, domesticating the disruptive potential of ethnography into institutional compliance (Rutgers DEWG, 2026).

## Contested Views: Building Around the Periphery

*Applying the "Adversarial Fact-Checking" protocol to the institutional model:*

### The "Museum of Digital Life" Risk
While DEWG documents digital cultures, it remains bound by the bureaucratic hierarchies of the university.
- **Fact-Check:** 2026 academic ethnography still relies on peer-review cycles that are slower than the "Magma" of CT and Farcaster.
- **Refute:** Rutgers DEWG is not studying the "future"; it is creating a **museum of the present.** It documents the decline of the old world while being unable to act within the new "Sovereign" world of Network States.

### The "Decoloniality" Performance
- **The Critique:** DEWG's 2026 focus on "Decolonial Practices" is contested by skeptics who argue that "digital ethnography" is fundamentally **data extraction** with an empathetic vocabulary. It fails to challenge the capitalist power of the platforms it studies.
