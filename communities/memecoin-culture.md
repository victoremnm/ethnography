# Memecoin Culture

## What It Is

Memecoins are cryptocurrencies with no intrinsic utility whose value is **entirely socially constructed through collective belief, attention, and narrative momentum**. They are the purest expression of crypto's attention economy mechanics. Where Bitcoin is digital gold and Ethereum is programmable money, memecoins are **programmable memes** — the financial instrument *is* the cultural object.

The current epicenter: **pump.fun** on Solana. As of January 2025, over 6 million tokens had been launched. Daily revenue hit $15.8M peak in January 2025. Platform took $700M+ in fees in 2024. This is not niche; this is mass-participation phenomenon.

---

## The Core Cultural Logic

> **Attention is the scarce resource. Tokens financialize attention.**

This is not irrational — it's an explicit acknowledgment that all asset value is ultimately socially constructed, and memecoins just strip away the pretense. The community understands this. It's a feature, not a bug.

BONKbot's internal documentation (Shitcoining 101, March 2025) encodes this as first-order participant belief: "Shitcoining = investing in attention" and "Narrative follows Price." The inversion of conventional finance logic — where price reflects fundamentals — is deliberate: in this model price *creates* narrative, which *creates* community, which generates buying pressure. A Girardian mimetic cascade in practitioner language.

### The pump.fun Mechanic
- Anyone can launch a token in ~2 minutes for ~$2
- **Bonding curve**: early buyers get cheapest price, token "graduates" to open market at market cap threshold (~$69K)
- Creates a **temporal lottery**: timing is everything, community momentum is everything
- First mover advantage is programmatically enforced
- **Critical operational note**: most participants filter and discover coins via **Photon Memescope**, not pump.fun's own interface — the launchpad and the discovery layer are separate ecosystems

### Status Currency
- **PnL screenshots** — most important social currency; must be on-chain verifiable
- **Early call receipts** — "I called $PNUT at 5k mcap" with contract proof
- **Follower count** — narrative reach proxy
- **Trenches longevity** — OG status matters; you've survived cycles

### Viral Mechanics (What Makes a Coin Pop)
1. **Emotional resonance** — real-world event that generates outrage or affection
   - $PNUT: squirrel euthanized by city officials → community outrage → coin
   - $CHILLGUY: relatable Gen Z vibe → TikTok crossover
2. **Aesthetic simplicity** — meme must be immediately "getable"
3. **Community momentum** — Telegram/Discord fills within hours
4. **TikTok/X crossover** — when normies discover it, early participants are already positioned
5. **On-chain narrative** — contract address shared, people check it, FOMO activates

### Metas Taxonomy (from BONKbot Shitcoining 101)
The community categorizes coins by their narrative type — the "meta" determines how a coin is evaluated and how narrative propagates:
- **Animal coins**: DOGE, WIF — universal emotional appeal, no political attachment required
- **Internet meme coins**: Pepe, Chud, Wojak — requires internet-native cultural literacy; skews toward chan/degen demographics; analytically significant for far-right cultural overlap
- **Famous-person-association coins**: DOGE/Elon, Broccoli/CZ — parasitic on existing celebrity attention; velocity depends on celebrity engagement

## Why People Do This

1. **Game theory**: Everyone knows most tokens fail. You're betting you can find/create/exit the 0.1% that succeeds before others do.
2. **Community belonging**: Shared suffering + shared wins create intense tribal bonds. The trenches produce in-group cohesion faster than most communities.
3. **Anti-establishment catharsis**: For asset-poor younger participants, a $500 PnL means something qualitatively different in Manila than in San Francisco. The "middle finger to wealth structures" framing is far more literal in SEA than in the US.

## The KOL Economy (The Real Mechanics)

"Paid or organic shilling" understates the operational reality. The KOL layer has a concrete price structure:

- **Pay-per-post rates**: established KOLs with 100K+ followers charge $500-5,000 per promotional tweet
- **Volume deals**: dev teams sign retainer arrangements for sustained promotion across a token's launch window
- **Coordinated pump operations**: multiple KOLs simultaneously posting about a token — functionally indistinguishable from securities fraud under any reasonable regulatory framework
- **The "organic" fiction**: the community knows most KOL calls are paid but maintains plausible deniability as a social norm. Calling this out is bad form; everyone knows and no one says it.

The operational implication: **a research service that can distinguish genuine KOL alpha from coordinated paid campaigns is extremely valuable** because this distinction determines whether a "call" is signal or noise. On-chain data (wallet relationship between KOL and token dev, timing of KOL purchases relative to promotion) is the only reliable way to detect this.

## The Geographic Distribution (The Missing Piece)

Memecoin culture has a pronounced Southeast Asia and Latin America component that changes the cultural analysis significantly:

- **Philippines, Indonesia, Vietnam**: significant retail participation, play-to-earn background (Axie Infinity created this cohort), acute awareness of wealth asymmetry
- **Brazil, Mexico, Argentina**: high crypto adoption driven by currency instability; memecoin speculation as currency hedge
- **The "middle finger to wealth structures" framing is far more literal in Manila than in San Francisco**: a $500 PnL means something qualitatively different depending on median income context

This geographic distribution also explains why some viral memes travel globally (emotional universals: squirrel, dog) while others die in their origin community (US political memes rarely resonate in Southeast Asia).

## The AI Agent Meta-Layer

A distinct sub-genre has emerged within the memecoin space: **tokens tied to AI agent personas**. The speculative thesis differs from standard memecoins — you're not buying attention momentum, you're buying belief in machine autonomy.

- **$ai16z**: peaked at $2B market cap; token named after the VC firm it satirized; community formed around the agent's "investment decisions"
- **Virtuals Protocol ($VIRTUAL)**: launchpad for AI agent tokens on Base; $37M DEX volume in 7 days (Feb 2026, Dune data) — confirms this is real trading volume, not just CT narrative
- **daos.fun**: agent DAO token launches; the pump.fun equivalent for agent tokens
- **The LOBSTAR incident (2025)**: AI agent given $50K SOL to trade autonomously; moved $442K — demonstrated how porous the human/machine boundary is in practice

Practically: most "autonomous" agents have human operators. The community maintains productive ambiguity about this. When the operator is caught interfering, community trust breaks quickly.

## What's Sticky vs. What Churns

**Stickiness signals:**
- Emotional anchoring to a real-world narrative
- Community-produced content (not just founder posts)
- Holder distribution that isn't too concentrated
- Regular holder growth + wallet age diversity

**Churn signals:**
- Single developer wallet holds >10% supply
- No organic community formation in 24-48 hours
- Launch without a legible meme or story
- Low-conviction buying (bots only)

## Contested Views: Building Around the Periphery

*While the primary narrative frames memecoins as a "democratization of finance" and "programmable memes," an adversarial lens reveals a different structural reality:*

### The "Extraction Machine" Reality
Memecoin culture is less an "attention economy" and more a **negative-sum extraction engine** designed to funnel retail capital to sophisticated insiders (Devs, KOLs, and MEV bots).
- **The ~1–2% Survival Rate:** On `pump.fun`, a small fraction of launched tokens ever reach the $69,000 "graduation" threshold to open markets — community on-chain analysis (see Storm Partners analysis in Sources) places this below 2%. The remaining tokens are "graveyard" tokens where the initial liquidity is absorbed by the platform and the deployer. *Verify exact rate against current pump.fun on-chain data via Dune before citing in external work.*
- **Forensic Evidence (Axiom Detection):** Using **Graph Visualization** and **Axiom Bundle Detection** tools, we can now prove that what appears to be "community buy-in" is often a single operator using 10-50 "bundled" wallets funded by a common SOL source.
- **The "Retail as Exit Liquidity" Pattern:** On-chain analysis consistently shows the majority of traders on launchpads are net-negative; cited estimates run as high as 95–98% net-negative. The precise figure depends on methodology (time window, wallet exclusions, fee accounting). The directional claim — most retail participants lose — is well-supported; the precise percentage is not independently audited. The "Wagmi" (We're All Gonna Make It) ritual is a linguistic survival mechanism used to maintain the influx of new "exit liquidity."
- **Institutionalized Rugging:** Critics describe the KOL Economy as a professionalized promotion-and-dump pipeline. KOLs often receive 1–5% of token supply (via stealth-launch participation or direct transfer) in exchange for a "call." Their promotional post creates the spike needed for them to exit their position. Regulatory analysts have compared this structure to undisclosed paid promotion under securities law — though no definitive legal determination has been made (see: [SEC guidance on crypto influencer disclosures, 2022](https://www.sec.gov/news/press-release/2022-183)).

### The "Anti-Establishment" Illusion
The "middle finger to wealth structures" narrative is the most effective marketing tool for a system that actually **reproduces wealth inequality** more efficiently than legacy systems. In the trenches, "asymmetric upside" is statistically a lottery with worse odds than a casino, packaged as a revolutionary political act.

## Glossary Addendum

*Operational vocabulary sourced primarily from BONKbot's Shitcoining 101 internal documentation (March 2025). Analytic notes where etymology is relevant to the ethnographic research.*

---

### Operational Terms

**Trenches**
The low-cap market environment where tokens trade below ~$50K market cap. Connotes extreme risk, constant adversarial pressure, and high mortality rate for positions. The term borrows military language deliberately — survival in the trenches is a status marker; it implies you have withstood conditions that eliminated others. Cross-reference with "trench longevity" as a status signal.

**Dev / Deployer**
The wallet that created and launched a coin. Not necessarily the project's public face — often anonymous. The dev/deployer relationship to the token's supply is the key forensic variable: how much did they retain, how much did they pre-buy before public launch, and have they sold.

**Bundle / Bundling**
Launching a token and simultaneously buying a significant portion of the supply using multiple wallets coordinated from a single source. This creates the appearance of community buying activity while a single actor controls supply. Axiom Detection's bundle visualization tool exists specifically to identify this pattern. Bundling is common enough that it has its own vocabulary; the community does not universally condemn it — some view it as a "dev protecting their work."

**Farm / Farming**
A structured extraction scam. A dev deploys a token, creates artificial activity (volume, wallet diversity), attracts retail buyers, then exits. Differs from a simple rug in that it involves more sophisticated social engineering over a longer time horizon.

**Crime**
Community shorthand for suspicious on-chain activity — possible money laundering, presale manipulation, or coordinated extraction. The term normalizes the observation without requiring legal precision. When a Rickbot audit surfaces "crime" signals, it typically refers to wallet clustering patterns consistent with laundering or coordinated presales.

**PA (Price Action)**
The price movement of a token over time. "Watching the PA" means monitoring whether momentum is up or down. A "PA call" is advice based on chart reading rather than fundamentals (which don't exist in this market).

**CA (Contract Address)**
The on-chain identifier for a token — the string of characters that uniquely identifies it on the blockchain. Sharing the CA is the first step in directing people to a token; it's the equivalent of a stock ticker in this ecosystem, but more precise (tickers can be duplicated; contract addresses cannot).

**Bonding Curve**
The pricing mechanism used by pump.fun: as more tokens are bought, price increases automatically according to a mathematical curve. There is zero initial liquidity required — the curve itself provides price discovery. Tokens "graduate" off the bonding curve onto open-market trading at approximately $69K market cap. The bonding curve mechanic is what enables the temporal lottery structure: early buyers get the cheapest prices by design.

**Clipping**
Selling a portion of one's position — taking partial profit while remaining exposed to further upside. The opposite of going "full stack." Clipping is considered the rational degen move; it extends the game theory horizon.

**Full Stacking**
Selling the entire position in one move. Also called "FSH" (see below). When a major holder full stacks, it creates visible price impact and can trigger a cascade of selling.

**TP (Take Profit)**
A pre-set price level at which a trader exits. Standard financial terminology used without modification.

**Cabal**
A group of wallets or traders acting in coordination — colluding to buy together, pump price, and exit together. In practice, cabals are difficult to distinguish from organic community formation except through on-chain forensics (wallet funding sources, transaction timing). The word is used matter-of-factly, not conspiratorially.

**FNF (Friends and Family)**
Early insider access to a token before public launch — the dev's social circle getting in at zero or near-zero cost. FNF wallets appear on-chain as early buyers with disproportionately large initial positions and are a key forensic signal for assessing dev extraction risk.

**Freshie**
A wallet with no prior on-chain transaction history. "Freshie wallets" are analytically significant in two directions: (1) a freshie buying a new token is suspicious — it may be a bundle wallet spun up for a single operation; (2) a freshie holding into later rounds may be a genuine new participant.

### Etymologically Significant Terms

*The following terms have racist or far-right etymological roots that are analytically significant for this ethnography. The community uses them without consistent acknowledgment of their origins. This linguistic overlap between degen culture and far-right internet culture is not coincidental — both communities developed in overlapping chan-adjacent online spaces (4chan, Reddit's early crypto communities) and share a vocabulary that encodes their shared cultural substrate.*

**FSH ("Full-Stack Hitler")**
Community acronym for selling one's entire position. The underlying phrase is a racist slur constructed from a Nazi reference — the term fuses far-right internet irony culture with trading slang in a way that naturalizes both. Its casual use in operational documentation (BONKbot's Shitcoining 101 defines it without flagging the etymology) is evidence that the degen community's cultural substrate includes far-right internet culture as a constitutive layer, not an edge case. Analytically: this is Thread 6 (NRx/Dark Enlightenment) cultural contamination appearing at the operational vocabulary layer of mainstream crypto culture.

**Jeet**
A term for someone who sells early — exits a position before the full gain materializes. Derived from a racist/casteist slur targeting South Asian (specifically Indian) people, rooted in the derogatory term "jeet" used in certain British-Indian colonial contexts, subsequently deployed as a slur in chan culture. The term entered crypto degen vocabulary through 4chan-adjacent communities where South Asian retail investors were stereotyped as panic-sellers. Its adoption as a neutral trading term (with no consistent acknowledgment of its origin) follows the same pattern as other slurs that have been "irony-laundered" through chan culture into mainstream internet usage. Analytically significant: the geographic distribution of memecoin retail participation (heavily Southeast and South Asian) makes the embedded racism of this term structurally relevant, not merely incidental.
