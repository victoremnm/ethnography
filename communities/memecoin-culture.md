# Memecoin Culture

## What It Is

Memecoins are cryptocurrencies with no intrinsic utility whose value is **entirely socially constructed through collective belief, attention, and narrative momentum**. They are the purest expression of crypto's attention economy mechanics. Where Bitcoin is digital gold and Ethereum is programmable money, memecoins are **programmable memes** — the financial instrument *is* the cultural object.

The current epicenter: **pump.fun** on Solana. As of January 2025, over 6 million tokens had been launched. Daily revenue hit $15.8M peak in January 2025. Platform took $700M+ in fees in 2024 (Wikipedia, 2025).

---

## The Core Cultural Logic

> **Attention is the scarce resource. Tokens financialize attention.**

This is not irrational — it's an explicit acknowledgment that all asset value is ultimately socially constructed, and memecoins just strip away the pretense. The community understands this. It's a feature, not a bug.

### The pump.fun Mechanic
- Anyone can launch a token in ~2 minutes for ~$2
- **Bonding curve**: early buyers get cheapest price, token "graduates" to Raydium at market cap threshold (~$69K) (Wikipedia, 2025).

---

## The AI Agent Meta-Layer

A distinct sub-genre has emerged within the memecoin space: **tokens tied to AI agent personas**.

- **$ai16z**: peaked at $2B market cap; token named after the VC firm it satirized (Zhu & Li, 2025).
- **The LOBSTAR incident (2025)**: AI agent given $50K SOL to trade autonomously; moved $442K — demonstrated how porous the human/machine boundary is in practice (CCN, 2025).

---

## Contested Views: Building Around the Periphery

### The "Extraction Machine" Reality
Memecoin culture is less an "attention economy" and more a **negative-sum extraction engine** designed to funnel retail capital to sophisticated insiders.
- **The 1.5% Survival Rate:** On `pump.fun`, fewer than **1.5%** of launched tokens ever reach the $69,000 "graduation" threshold. The remaining 98.5% are "graveyard" tokens (Storm Partners, 2025).
- **Forensic Evidence:** Data suggests that **98.2% of traders** on launchpads are net-negative (CryptoSlate, 2025).
