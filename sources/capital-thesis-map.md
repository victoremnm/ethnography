# Capital & Intelligence Thesis Map

Who is funding and shaping the AI/crypto intersection — and what argument each player is making. This is not a directory; it's a **thesis map**: each row represents a distinct intellectual position on what the AI × crypto convergence is and who should capture the value.

The two communities (AI labs, crypto VCs) are currently on a collision course. OpenClaw banned crypto from its Discord. Anthropic enforced trademark against "Clawd." Meanwhile, crypto's agent token layer (Virtuals, ai16z, Clanker) is explicitly trying to become the identity/reputation/payment layer these AI frameworks plug into. Mapping both sides is necessary to understand who wins.

## The Defining Argument (2026)

Two incompatible futures are being built simultaneously:

**Crypto-native agent stack** (a16z, Virtuals, ai16z, Clanker): Crypto provides identity (ERC-8004), payments (x402), and reputation for AI agents. The agent economy runs on-chain. Token holders capture value.

**Centralized agent stack** (OpenAI payments, Anthropic identity, Google Cloud): AI labs build their own identity and payment infrastructure. Agents authenticate through existing OAuth. No tokens needed.

**The fork point**: Whether mainstream agent frameworks (OpenClaw, future products from OpenAI/Anthropic) adopt crypto rails or build competing centralized alternatives. OpenClaw's anti-crypto signal (Feb 23) is the first concrete evidence that the centralized stack is winning the user-facing layer, even as crypto infrastructure is being built.

**Vitalik's counter-argument**: Cryptographic constraints (smart contracts as access control) are not ideological preferences — they are the only trustless mechanism for defining what an agent can touch. If Claude Opus 4.6 exhibits misalignment (Anthropic's own risk report), you don't want to connect it to your bank account. You want to load it with $50 in on-chain tokens that define exactly its operational scope.
