# Where the Puck Is Going: Strategic Synthesis

**Audience**: Stanford MS&E grad, engineering manager background, 30s, wants to understand emerging communities and identify what to build to participate in this ecosystem as a VC researcher or product builder.

**Method**: Synthesized from ethnographic profiles of AI maxxers/e/acc, memecoin culture, prediction markets, a16z ecosystem, cEDH community, TikTok creators (in progress), BJJ community (in progress). Evaluated through MTG/data community/Stanford lenses.

## The One-Sentence Summary

**Attention is the scarce resource, narrative is the asset, and the people who can translate between on-chain behavioral data and human-readable intelligence will capture the most value in the next 5 years.**

## The Structural Shift Happening Right Now

Three things are converging simultaneously:

### 1. AI Agents Are Becoming Economic Actors
- Autonomous agents with wallets are no longer hypothetical — they are live in production on Solana and other chains
- The $ai16z / Virtuals ecosystem peaked at $2B market cap for tokens tied to AI agent personas
- Stripe co-founder: "a torrent of AI agent commerce powered by stablecoins" — this is 2-3 years away from being mundane
- **What this creates**: a market for infrastructure, for identity systems, for "agent-native" products that assume the consumer is a machine, not a human
- **What it disrupts**: anything whose business model depends on a human making a decision at the point of transaction

### 2. The Regulatory Environment Is Being Actively Written Right Now
- This is the most time-sensitive window. Frameworks are being written in real time. Post-2024, the US administration is sympathetic to crypto; the regulations that pass in the next 18-24 months will define the next decade of the industry.
- a16z is writing these frameworks alongside government actors. Founders with DC relationships now have structural advantages they didn't have 3 years ago.
- **What this means**: products that are viable today may be regulated out of existence, and products that are currently illegal may become the default in 18 months. The companies being built now are making bets on specific regulatory outcomes.
- **The opportunity for a researcher**: understanding what regulatory bets the ecosystem is making is more valuable than understanding what technology bets it's making

### 3. The Translation Layer Is Underbuilt
- VCs cannot read Dune Analytics. They cannot parse Telegram pump group behavior. They cannot track wallet clustering.
- Data engineers can build these analyses but don't have VC relationships or the narrative translation ability to make them useful to institutional audiences.
- **The gap**: between on-chain behavioral signal (what's actually happening) and investor-digestible narrative (what it means for deals)

## The Communities and Where They're Going

### AI Maxxers / e/acc
**Where it's going**: The "e/acc" label is being abandoned but the underlying worldview is accelerating. The people who held this ideology are now in government, in regulatory positions, at the heads of YC cohorts. The ideology has been institutionalized even as the brand has been diluted.

**The puck**: The next form of this community is **AI-native founders** who don't use the e/acc label but operate by its logic: ship first, ask for forgiveness later, treat safety concerns as a competitor's FUD. They're in stealth companies building with Claude/GPT-4o APIs right now. The network around YC's 2025-2026 cohorts is where to look.

**What to build**: Tools for AI-native founders that assume agency augmentation (not replacement), that integrate into existing engineering workflows, and that solve the specific pain of shipping AI products at speed (evals, monitoring, deployment pipelines). This community buys infrastructure, not platforms.

### Memecoin Culture
**Where it's going**: The "pure speculation" layer will increasingly merge with "AI agent-launched" tokens. The $ai16z phenomenon — a token tied to an AI agent's persona — points toward a future where memecoins are launched by AIs, traded by AIs, and the human participation is spectatorship and curation rather than origination.

**The geographic shift**: The center of gravity is moving from US-dominant to Southeast Asia and LATAM-dominant participation. Products that assume English-language, US-timezone, high-discretionary-income users are missing most of the actual market.

**The puck**: The next memecoin cycle will be **narrative-driven by AI agents with persistent personas** — tokens tied to characters that post, interact, "have opinions." The community building around these agents (fandoms, essentially) will be the new memecoin community. You're watching the emergence of AI-native parasocial relationships with financial instruments attached.

**What to build**: Either (a) the infrastructure for agent identity/persistence (wallet + social layer for AI agents), or (b) the analytics layer that tells you whether a "viral" memecoin has organic community formation or manufactured momentum. Both are defensible. The first is bigger; the second is buildable with existing skills.

### Prediction Markets
**Where it's going**: Post-2024 election mainstreaming + regulatory clarity (CFTC approval for Polymarket US return) means prediction markets are entering the institutional phase. The next form is **prediction markets embedded in financial products** — not just "bet on whether the Fed raises rates" but rate expectations markets integrated into interest rate derivative hedging.

**The AI intersection**: LLMs are going to become prediction market participants. The question of "how do LLM forecasts compare to human Superforecasters" is being studied right now and will have commercial implications for risk management, media, and insurance.

**The puck**: Prediction markets as **embedded intelligence layer** in existing financial and media products. Not standalone platforms but APIs that provide probabilistic estimates embedded in Bloomberg terminals, news sites, insurance underwriting.

**What to build**: Calibration analytics for prediction market participants (who is actually well-calibrated vs. lucky), or the API layer that makes prediction market prices accessible for integration. The Metaculus community is actively looking for both.

### a16z Ecosystem
**Where it's going**: a16z's power is now more regulatory than financial. The $7.6B+ in crypto funds is enormous but their real moat is DC relationships and the ability to define what's legal. This is a 2-3 year competitive advantage that will erode as other VCs build comparable relationships.

**The internal shift**: The crypto team's current thesis is AI + blockchain infrastructure. Not DeFi speculation; not NFTs; not gaming. The bet is: AI agents need programmable money, identity, and compute. The companies being funded in 2026 are building those three things.

**The puck**: a16z will back **2-3 companies in 2026 that become the foundational infrastructure for AI agent economic activity**. These companies don't exist publicly yet or are in stealth. The way to identify them: follow the a16z partners' public writing and track which technical primitives they're referencing as "unsolved."

**What to build for the VC audience**: The intelligence product the evaluator identified — narrative-to-deal-flow translation. "This memecoin narrative cluster implies these founders will be pitching in 8 weeks. Here's the specific thesis they'll use." This is defensible, differentiated, and doesn't require a16z relationships to start.

## The Geographic Distribution Map

This was identified as a missing piece across multiple community profiles. Here's the synthesis:

| Community | US Center | SEA/LATAM Center | Significance |
|-----------|-----------|-----------------|--------------|
| e/acc / AI maxxers | San Francisco / NYC | Minimal | Primarily a US/European ideology export |
| Memecoin degens | US participation, but declining | Philippines, Indonesia, Brazil, Mexico, Nigeria | Majority of retail volume is non-US |
| Prediction markets | US-dominant | UK, India emerging | US political markets dominate volume |
| a16z ecosystem | Menlo Park + NYC | Singapore, Dubai (capital flight) | Capital concentrated in US but deployment is global |
| cEDH | US, Brazil | Japan, Europe | Brazilian origin carries cultural weight |
| TikTok creators | US (primary commercial market) | Southeast Asia (primary user base) | Creator monetization is US-centric; audience is global |
| BJJ | Brazil (origin/prestige), US (commercial) | Global but origin matters | Brazilian lineage = credential inflation |

**The pattern**: money and narrative originate in US (and specifically SF/NYC). Retail participation and actual usage are global, concentrated in the Global South. Products that serve only the US narrative layer are missing most of the human activity. Products that translate between the two layers are underbuilt.

## The Historian's Formula

An Oxford/Cambridge historian looking at this landscape would apply the following analytical frame that cuts across all these communities:

> **Every major technological transition produces a speculative layer that precedes the productive layer by 5-10 years, and the productive layer captures most of the durable value.**

- Railroad bubble (1840s) → railroad companies mostly failed; the real winners were the industries enabled by cheap transport (mail order, grain markets)
- Internet bubble (1999) → most dot-coms failed; AWS, Google, and mobile ate the value
- Crypto (2017 ICO boom, 2021 NFT/DeFi boom) → most tokens failed; the infrastructure (Ethereum, Solana, USDC) is what persisted
- AI (2023-2025 AI startup bubble) → most AI wrappers will fail; the productive layer is the infrastructure and the applications with genuine distribution moats

**The implication for your positioning**: you're watching the speculative layer. The productive layer is being built by people who aren't on crypto Twitter. The skill that survives is the ability to identify *which* speculative bets are pointing toward durable productive value — and to communicate that analysis to the people allocating capital.

That is the intelligence product worth building.
