## The Anthropic/Pentagon Scandal: A Timeline of Contradictions

### 1. The Federal Ban (February 27, 2026)
President Donald Trump issued an executive order prohibiting all federal agencies from using **Anthropic's Claude AI**. Secretary of War Pete Hegseth designated the company a **"Supply-Chain Risk to National Security,"** accusing CEO Dario Amodei of "ideological betrayal" for refusing to remove safety guardrails that prevented the model from assisting in lethal autonomous strikes (Hegseth, 2026).

### 2. The Operational Breach (February 28 – March 1, 2026)
Despite the ban, reports from *The Wall Street Journal* revealed that **U.S. Central Command (CENTCOM)** relied on Claude 4.6 to orchestrate the strikes in Iran. The military justified this breach by claiming the AI was so deeply **"embedded"** in the classified tactical networks that immediate withdrawal would have caused a catastrophic failure of the mission (CENTCOM, 2026).

### 3. The OpenAI Pivot
Following the blacklisting of Anthropic, the Department of War signed an emergency multi-billion dollar deal with **OpenAI**. The agreement specified that OpenAI's models would be "unrestricted" for all lawful military purposes, signaling a decisive victory for the "Any Lawful Use" faction over the "Constitutional AI" movement (OpenAI, 2026).

## Contested Views: Building Around the Periphery

*Applying the Adversarial Fact-Checking protocol to the Military-Agent convergence:*

### The "Accountability Gap"
While the military touts the efficiency of Agentic Warfare, it ignores the looming **"Black Box Accountability"** crisis.
- **Fact-Check:** 2026 Army War College tests showed that LLMs struggle with geospatial reasoning, leading to a 14% higher "collateral hallucination" rate in simulated urban combat.
- **Refute:** Operation Epic Fury was an "intelligence success," but it sets a dangerous precedent: **Who is responsible if an autonomous agent misidentifies a civilian target?** The current legal framework provides no mechanism for prosecuting an "algorithmic error" (NIST, 2026).

### The "Sovereignty" Paradox
The U.S. military is currently "sovereign" only as long as it has access to private corporate models (OpenAI/Anthropic).
- **The Risk:** If a private company's infrastructure fails (e.g., the March 1st AWS outage), the military's primary command layer disappears.
- **Conclusion:** We are seeing the rise of **"Corporate-Military Dependency,"** where the nation-state is functionally subservient to the uptime of a few "Silicon Empires" (Srnicek, 2025).
